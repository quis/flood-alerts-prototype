What we will do

We will try to send the flood warning you have asked for. When we do that, we'll do what is reasonable, but we can't guarantee it will be sent or arrive. Warnings may be sent at any time of the day or night.

We do not:

  • accept responsibility if you fail to receive a warning
  • guarantee that our website or the phone lines will be available
  • accept responsibility for any loss, damage or costs incurred by you caused by flooding, or by issuing or failing to issue warnings - except where the law says we must

You are responsible for:

  • making sure that the contact details you give us are correct
  • telling us about any changes

We may use the personal information you give us to:

  • send you the warnings you have signed up for
  • send you a small number of service announcements or administrative messages
  • help emergency services and local councils respond to flooding
  • help with our work on flood warning and flood risk management
  • respond to freedom of information requests (if data protection law allows it)

We may give your information to our agents or representatives, so they can do any of these things for us. And we may share your information with other organisations if the law says we must.

The Environment Agency manages the flood warning systems. Their personal information charter (opens new window) explains how they deal with your information. Natural Resources Wales use the same systems and will have access to your personal information if you ask for a service in Wales.
